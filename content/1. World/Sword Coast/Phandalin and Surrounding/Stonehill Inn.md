# Stonehill Inn

> In the center of town stands a large, newly built roadhouse of fieldstone and rough-hewn timbers. The common room is filled with locals nursing mugs of ale or cider, all of them eyeing you with curiosity. 

## Story so far

This is the first place you visited when arriving at Phandalin. It's not a large inn by any standards, but accommodates all the social needs of such a quaint and quiet town, enough for you to feel rested after a long, goblin-filled day.

The innkeeper's name is [[Toblen Stonehill]], a friendly young human male. Toblen is a native of the town of Triboar to the east of Phandalin. He came to Phandalin to prospect, but soon realized that he knew a lot more about running an inn than he did about mining.

Toblen is upset that the [Redbrands](Redbrands%20Ruffians.md) have been allowed to terrorize the town, and that [[Harbin Wester]], the townmaster, has done nothing to curtail them. However, he tries not to stir up trouble for fear that the Redbrands might retaliate against his wife and children.

At the inn, you heard all kinds of rumors of the town and the surroundings. Particularly, there are several places that you deemed worth investigating:
+ [[Edermath Orchard]]: Rumor is that the owner is a retired adventurer who could offer some insights and guidance.
+ [[Shrine of Luck]]: Sister Garaele, who oversees the Shrine of Luck, recently left town for a few days, then returned wounded and exhausted.
+ [[Townmaster's Hall]]: [[Harbin Wester]], the townmaster, is looking for someone to deal with the orc raiders ravaging Triboar Trail, an east-west trail to the north of Phandalin
+ [[Alderleaf Farm]]: [[Qelline Alderleaf]]'s son, Carp, discovered a small tunnel in the woods next to town, but the Redbrands almost caught him.
+ [[Phandalin's Miner Exchange]]: The Redbrands hassle every business in town, except for the Phandalin Miner's Exchange. They don't want trouble with [[Halia Thornton]], who runs it.
